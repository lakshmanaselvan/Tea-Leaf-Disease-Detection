Glance

Tea, as a perennial crop under the monoculture system, stands in the environment for years and experiences numerous agronomic practices throughout its lifespan. The tea plant encounters many seasonal (blister blight, gray blight, and brown blight) as well as perennial diseases (canker and wood rots) during its lifespan. In annual crops, diseases are managed by tillage, rotation, and delayed seeding/sowing that disrupt the disease cycle. However, such practices are not feasible for the perennial tea system. The occurrence of disease, extent of damage caused, and the strategies adopted for disease management are generally specific for each tea-growing country. Integrated disease management (IDM), including the use of cultural, chemical, and biological methods and host plant resistance, practiced in the global tea industry, is discussed here.
